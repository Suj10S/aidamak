Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about studying in Japan

To study in Japan, you typically need: completed high school education (for undergraduate) or bachelor's degree (for graduate), basic Japanese language proficiency (JLPT N5-N2 depending on program), financial proof showing ability to cover tuition and living expenses, and a clean academic record. Some programs also accept students with English proficiency for English-taught courses.

The total cost varies by university and location. While many programs require Japanese proficiency, there are increasing numbers of English-taught programs (called "Global Programs") available at major universities. However, basic Japanese knowledge is highly recommended for daily life, part-time work, and better integration. The student visa process typically takes 3-6 months total. First, the university applies for your Certificate of Eligibility (COE) which takes 1-3 months. Once you receive the COE, the actual visa application at the Japanese embassy/consulate takes 5-10 working days. We recommend starting the process 6 months before your intended departure date to ensure smooth processing.
